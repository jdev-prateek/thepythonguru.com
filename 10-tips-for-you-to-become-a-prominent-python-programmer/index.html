10 Tips for You to Become a Prominent Python Programmer

Updated on Jan 07, 2020

Python may not be the most used programming language on the web, but it is well designed and has many features that make newcomers choose, and established developers switch, to Python. It is constantly growing and compared to other languages such as Java and C++, Python's easy syntax and readability make learning it a lot easier. The Python language has a variety of uses in the software field, but developers are mostly dealing with backend components, connecting applications and giving support to frontend developers in web applications. Recently, many Python programmers have also joined mobile app development teams to improve the use of this language in mobile computing.

Numerous websites offer Python programmers for hire and whether you decide to sign up with one of these as a freelancer or want to join a remote development team, there are plenty of opportunities to explore. The question also often arises: How much do Python programmers make? The average Python salary for programmers is above average. Therefore, many strive to gain these skills. With the rising popularity of Python programming language, the demand for Python programmers is also high, while the availability of highly skilled programmers is low. This creates an environment with plenty of opportunities. So many wonders, ‘How to become a prominent Python programmer’? If you have a desire to be part of the global market of Python developers, there are a few steps you can follow to assist you on the journey.

Be committed to becoming a Python programmer #

To become a Python developer, you need to commit to being patient while you study the language and you must persist in your journey. Learning a programming language is demanding and you will need to be dedicated to continual exposure to code to ensure you keep your knowledge and skills relevant.

Become the programming student #

Like with any programming language, it is important to learn as much as you can. Explore all there is to the Python language, find out about its history, ask many questions and learn as much as you can from established developers.

Practice – code every day! #

Like with everything: Practice makes perfect. When you are learning a new language, it's important to practice as often as you can. Remember that Python has quite a higher percentage of newcomers than many other languages. According to Stack Overflow data 30% of developers have no more than two years' experience in a working environment. The competition for high-end jobs are high, so it is essential to practice daily to keep up with the latest trends.

Join others who are also learning #

Coding is often seen as an activity best practised in solidarity, but it is important to surround yourself with others who are also learning during the beginning of your journey. If you are not able to find others in your community, try to join a Python development team online, such as PythonistaCafe. PythonistaCafe is a growing online community of Python enthusiasts helping each other succeed and grow. Joining a group of like-minded peers will allow you to learn from each other and to share advice.

Take breaks when you need to #

While you are still learning, you need to set specific times to take time out to absorb new theories and ideas. Do not allow yourself to get lost in hours of trying to figure something out. When you are dealing with a lot of new information, make sure you take study breaks often.

Teach others #

Often the best way to learn something is to teach others. When you are learning Python, be sure to teach what you have learned on the way by writing informative articles, sharing tips through short videos, joining online programming forums and just talking to others who are also on the road to becoming Python programmers. When you explain something out loud, you often help yourself to remember concepts.

Ask questions often #

During your entire journey of Python programming, you want to make sure you ask a lot of questions. You can also write down questions and answers to use in the future. Asking questions will help you to improve your thought process and your communication skills, which is meaningful once you are working as a programmer.

Build something new #

Start with small exercises when you are at the beginning of your journey. This way, you will become confident quickly as you use the concepts you've already learned. As soon as you are confident enough with some data structures, try to build something. The building will teach you the most and will be a clear indication of where you are on your path to becoming a prominent programmer.

Keep track of your changes #

It's important to keep track of every change you make to a file. The sooner you come into the habit of doing this, the easier it will be for you once you are employed. Tip: Most jobs list this as a requirement!

Keep your programming skills relevant #

With the rise of tech jobs, many are looking to enter the programming field. This is why it is crucial to keep your knowledge and skills updated. You want to ensure that you are always in the know when it comes to the latest tricks and trends.

The journey to becoming a Python programmer is not always a smooth one but if you are committed, there are many job options to explore. According to The Economist, Python is becoming the world’s most popular coding language and the opportunities in the field are endless. With Python, you can become a Python Coder, a Python Web Developer, Software Engineer, Data Analyst, Data Scientist or an Automation tester, etc. And hence, the Python language can open a lot of doors for you.

If you are ready to dive into the world of Python, it's better to start sooner than later. Keep in mind that it is important to keep your skills relevant and to practice often to ensure you are always on top of your game.
